This course is intended to introduce students to Information System security approaches based on System-State models, as opposed to the more prevalent approach of relying on Attack Models.

While several system-state models exist, not enough importance is given to reliable EXECUTION of models. This course will introduce students to modeling strategies that permit reliable execution within resource limited environments.

The course will include a combination of lectures by the instructor and presentations by students. After students are introduced to strategies for designing system models for information systems, they will be required to design models for practical real-life information systems of their choice (for example, specific systems like Facebook, Twitter, Dropbox, or generic systems for Auctions, Voting, etc.)

Students are expected to submit a one-page overview of every system to be discussed in the class. Following the presentation, every student will be required to submit a two-page extended overview. One-page reviews will be accepted only in the classroom (as hardcopies)Extended reviews should be submitted by Email in PDF format ( only PDF format will be accepted )

ADD-DROP POLICY

  1. Add/drop without penalty

    A student has through the fifth class day into the semester to add a course and through the tenth class day to drop a course without being assessed a fee or academic penalty.

  2. Drop after the tenth class day through the 30th class day into the semester

    A student who elects to drop a course during this period must receive the approval of his/her adviser, will be assigned a W on his/her academic record, and be assessed a fee. The adviser who permits the drop will specify its effective date.

  3. Drop after the 30th class day into the semester

    A student cannot drop courses after this period except in documented cases of serious illness, extreme hardship, or failure of the instructor to provide significant assessment of his/her performance. A request to drop a course during this period must be approved by the student's adviser and academic dean. The dean who permits the drop will specify its effective date. A student receiving permission to drop will receive a W on his/her academic record and be assessed a fee.

  4. Faculty are expected to provide a student with significant evidence or assessment of his/her class performance within the first six weeks.

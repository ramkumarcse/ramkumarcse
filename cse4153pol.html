Attendance

All students (including auditors) are required to attend all classes, and to present excuses for justifiable absences (e.g., attending a conference, or serious illness). This includes classes given by guest lecturers.

Makeup for missed tests will only be offered if scheduled in advance, unless the reason for the absence is documented by a physician or a dean.

Academic Honesty (very important)

The departmental policy is included here by implication, and students taking this course are expected to read it before submitting the first assignment. All assignments will provide instructions as to the permissible forms of collaboration. The instructor must report any infraction to the appropriate academic authorities.

The first infraction on an assignment/quiz/exam will result in an automatic zero (0) points for that assignment/quiz/exam. Repeated infractions will result in an 'F' grade for the course. Additional penalties may be assessed through the academic honesty system of MSU.

It is your responsibility to understand and follow the academic policy rules. If in doubt, cite the source, or ask your instructor in advance.

ADD-DROP POLICY

  1. Add/drop without penalty

    A student has through the fifth class day into the semester to add a course and through the tenth class day to drop a course without being assessed a fee or academic penalty.

  2. Drop after the tenth class day through the 30th class day into the semester

    A student who elects to drop a course during this period must receive the approval of his/her adviser, will be assigned a W on his/her academic record, and be assessed a fee. The adviser who permits the drop will specify its effective date.

  3. Drop after the 30th class day into the semester

    A student cannot drop courses after this period except in documented cases of serious illness, extreme hardship, or failure of the instructor to provide significant assessment of his/her performance. A request to drop a course during this period must be approved by the student's adviser and academic dean. The dean who permits the drop will specify its effective date. A student receiving permission to drop will receive a W on his/her academic record and be assessed a fee.

  4. Faculty are expected to provide a student with significant evidence or assessment of his/her class performance within the first six weeks.

Incomplete Grade

A grade of I ("incomplete") will not be granted EXCEPT under those circumstances allowed by university policy. Approval must be sought from the Computer Science department head for any exceptions to this policy.
